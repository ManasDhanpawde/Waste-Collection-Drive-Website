What is E-waste

The term e-waste refers to electronic products that have reached the end of their useful lives. Mobile phones, tablets, laptops, video game consoles, cameras and much more constitute modern e-waste in addition to desktops, speakers, television sets, VCRs, stereos, copiers and fax machines.

Why Donate E-waste

Electronic waste is of hazardous nature. It may pose risk to our health and environment if not disposed off correctly. It contains Lead, mercury, cadmium, Beryllium, Barium and chromium which can damage human health and pollute the environment, if not disposed off and recycled properly.

How does it work?

When you donate your used electronics to us, we will take care of everything else. We will dispose off all the hazardous materials safely. We refurbish computers and other electronics and donate them to the needy free of charge.

How to handle e-waste: Do's and Don't of E-waste Management

E-waste is becoming a global issue. The more electrical and electronic equipment are being produced, the more e-waste need to be disposed or managed properly. Here's what you should and should not do while handling e-garbage on your own.

Do's

Reduce

  • Use electrical Appliances until the end of product life.
  • Think before buying additional new electronic appliances.

Re-use

  • Use pre-loved appliances, toys etc.
  • Purchase refurbished recycled electrical products.

Recycle

  • Separate your e-waste from domestic waste.
  • Send your e-waste ONLY to registered collectors for disposal.

Don'ts

  • Simply burn or dump in the garbage bins.
  • Dismantle your electronic appliances on yourself
  • Dispose to the illegal landfill / informal sector
